Title of First Image

Race, ethnicity, and individual and neighborhood-level socio-economic status are important to consider when delivering mental health care for people with multiple sclerosis. Racial and ethnic disparities are linked to critical differences in social determinants of health. This includes socio-economic status. Low socio-economic status has been associated with greater burden of comorbidities with multiple sclerosis, a disease in which the immune system eats away at the protective covering of the nerves, disparities in symptom management, and potentially greater risk for disability progression. It is hypothesized that people with multiple sclerosis with lower socio-econimic status are more likely to experience symptoms of anxiety, depression, and fatigue compared to those who have higher socio-economic status and that these differences are modified by racial and ethnic backgrounds. In the end, it was confirmed that higher socio-economic status was in fact associated with lower burden of psychiatric symptoms but with a higher likelihood of self-reported symptom recovery after receiving mental health treatment.

Title of Image 5

According to some studies, there is an increase in recognition that children's early years are very critical as social inequities in their mental health can develop. A study has shown that a socio-economic gradient exists in children from 5 to 18 years old and impacts their mental health development. A study was done in which they analyzed the child's behavior and how they externalized their problems for this they measured the child's behavior with a checklist. The different variables that showed an impact mainly were the level of maternal education at the gestation of 20 weeks. The results were that Children of mothers in the most educated quintile scored 11% lower for externalizing problems compared to children of mothers in the least educated quintile and the ones in the least educated quantile were showing a higher percentage of having children with mental health problems. This study was able to evidence how children that are at a social disadvantage are more likely to suffer from mental illnesses as their mothers lack maternal education leading to this happening.

As we know bullying has a great impact on individual mental health, Bullying is a very common problem that occurs but the chance of a person suffering from it is also influenced by their social economic status. There was a survey made between two different economic classes of children about bullying and it showed that about the age of seven about 47.8% are bullied. But there was a change between the incomes as the ones with the lowest incomes showed that about 53.4% are bullied while the ones with higher income showed 43.9% being bullied. This would evidence the impact that social-economic factors have on bullying meaning that the ones that are at most risk are the ones with lower income, also increasing their probability of having a mental illness.
